Sydney Opera House

The Sydney Opera House is one of the most iconic architectural works of the 20th century.Its unique roof top consists of over 1 million Swedish roof tiles, eaching covering about 4 acres. The entire construction of the building took 14 years, whereas it was expected to be constructed by 4 years. In addition, it is one of the busiest performing arts centre in the world with more than 10.9 million people visiting the venue every year, 200,000 of which take a guided tour of the complex each year. The centre features theatre, musicals, opera, contemporary dance, ballet, and various forms of music, with around 3,000 events each year.Inside the building, there are nearly 1000 rooms including 5 main auditoria along with a Reception Hall, five rehearsal studios, four restaurants, six theatre bars, extensive foyer and lounge areas, sixty dressing rooms and suites, library, an artists' lounge and canteen known as the "Green Room", administrative offices and extensive plant and machinery areas.
